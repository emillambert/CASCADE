# CASCADE unit economics assumptions

This note documents the geography-expansion business model used in the paper and generated by `unit_economics.py`.

## Core pricing assumptions

- Product model: per-hectare analytics sold through existing farm-platform distribution
- CASCADE alerting price: `$4.00/ha/year`
- Platform take rate: `20%`
- Variable service cost: `$0.35/ha/year`
- Carbon MRV add-on: `$0.50/ha/year` on `50%` of enrolled hectares from `Y2` onward

That yields:

```text
Alert-only contribution margin = $4.00 * (1 - 0.20) - $0.35 = $2.85/ha/year
Alert + MRV contribution margin = $2.85 + ($0.50 * 0.50) = $3.10/ha/year
```

## Primary model: five-year geography expansion

The paper-facing story is not that the San Joaquin Valley is the entire market. The SJV is the validation anchor because it is where the real MODIS replay is run. The commercial model then scales across larger irrigated geographies without changing the CASCADE onboard architecture.

The paper uses the **low/design-to-cost** hosted-payload case as the headline scenario:

| Year | Milestone | Geography | Coverage | Revenue | Op. margin |
| --- | --- | --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| `Y1` | SJV pilot | California SJV | `3.5% SJV` | `$0.20M` | `grant-funded` |
| `Y2` | California scale | California | `28.3% SJV` | `$1.70M` | `+17.1%` |
| `Y3` | U.S. national | U.S. irrigated | `11.0% US` | `$10.62M` | `+60.4%` |
| `Y4` | EU + Brazil entry | EU + Brazil | `35.1% EU+Brazil` | `$27.62M` | `+68.1%` |
| `Y5` | Global platform | Global | `6.0% global` | `$76.50M` | `+70.7%` |

Distribution path:

- `Y1`: direct paid pilot with one irrigation-district-scale customer in the SJV
- `Y2`: California rollout through Climate FieldView plus direct enterprise sales
- `Y3`: U.S. expansion through Climate FieldView and John Deere Operations Center
- `Y4`: EU and Brazil entry through xarvio and John Deere Brazil
- `Y5`: global multi-platform distribution including CropX

## Fixed-cost sensitivity

The model still retains low, base, and high fixed-cost cases as supporting evidence. These are explicit scenario assumptions, not quoted Loft price sheets.

| Scenario | Single-satellite annual fixed cost | Interpretation |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Low | `$0.95M` | Design-to-cost hosted payload plus lean support stack |
| Base | `$1.50M` | Mid-case hosted payload, integration, and support |
| High | `$2.30M` | More conservative hosted payload and support burden |

Additional constellation capacity is modeled with step-up multipliers:

- `Y1`: `1.0x`
- `Y2`: `1.0x`
- `Y3`: `1.4x`
- `Y4`: `1.4x`
- `Y5`: `1.8x`

That keeps the main story simple while preserving the sensitivity picture:

- Low/design-to-cost case breaks even in `Y2` at `400k ha`
- Base case breaks even in `Y3` at `2.5M ha`
- High case also breaks even in `Y3` at `2.5M ha`

## Market references used in the model

- San Joaquin Valley pilot geography: `1.415M ha`
- U.S. irrigated land baseline: `22.7M ha`
- EU irrigated agriculture baseline: `11.0M ha`
- Brazil irrigated agriculture baseline: `7.5M ha`
- Global irrigated agriculture baseline: `300M ha`

These market references are written into `artifacts/economics/unit_economics.json` alongside the per-scenario milestone results.

## Interpretation

The purpose of this model is judge-facing clarity, not investor-style downside diligence. It is meant to show:

- how CASCADE moves from a replay-anchored SJV pilot to a real MVP
- when it reaches first credible commercial scale
- why the same architecture can generalize internationally without a new payload design

The retained sensitivity data remain useful, but they are supporting evidence. The primary commercial story is the five-year rollout from `Y1` pilot to `Y5` global platform.
