\section{Expected Next Steps}

MASFE is currently a TRL 3 concept: the scheduling logic, fusion equation, and SWAP closure are demonstrated in software, but the payload has not yet been exercised on target hardware. The next milestone is a TRL 4 hardware-in-the-loop demonstration on a LEON4FT-class evaluation platform using real EarthData scenes. After that, the cleanest TRL 5 path is a hosted-payload pilot through Loft Orbital, because it validates the business model and the operations concept at the same time.

If selected as a finalist, the highest-value actions before the June pitch event are straightforward:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item run the simulation on real MODIS tiles and show the policy decisions live;
  \item turn the hosted-payload concept into a first-pass unit-economics model with Loft pricing and farm-platform pricing;
  \item validate the alert-latency and pricing assumptions through farmer interviews and one agronomy advisor review.
\end{enumerate}

The submission's main claim is therefore not that MASFE is flight-ready today. It is that the adaptive-sensing architecture is already clear, quantitative, and commercially legible enough to justify the next round of validation.
