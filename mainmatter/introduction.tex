\chapter{Introduction}
\label{chapter:introduction}

MASFE (Multi-Algorithm Scheduling and Fusion Engine) is an Orbital Edge AI system that addresses NASA’s challenge to \emph{“orchestrate existing land observation algorithms into an efficient, responsive onboard intelligence layer”}~\cite{nasa-challenge-brief}. A reinforcement-learning-inspired MDP scheduler on a radiation-tolerant LEON4FT CPU dynamically allocates compute across MODIS MOD13A1 (EVI) and MOD11A1 (LST), fused onboard via an FPGA into a Crop Stress Composite (CSC). The system makes real-time sensing, processing, and downlink decisions, delivering sub-90-minute crop risk alerts while reducing data downlink by 97\,\%. It is deployed as a hosted payload on a Loft Orbital YAM platform, integrating with OpenET~\cite{openet-api} for irrigation and disease advisory services.

Emil Wes Lambert is a BSc Aerospace Engineering student at TU Delft. Relevant coursework includes spacecraft systems engineering, AOCS, mission analysis, and embedded flight software. MASFE’s core challenge is partitioning computation between an FPGA and a radiation-tolerant CPU under SWAP constraints.
